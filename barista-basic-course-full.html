1. Coffee Introduction

Understand the history, origins, and journey of coffee. Learn the difference between Arabica and Robusta beans, processing methods (washed, natural, honey), and how altitude and region affect flavor.

2. Understanding Coffee Beans

  • Arabica: High-quality, sweeter, grown at high altitudes.
  • Robusta: Strong, bitter, more caffeine, grown at lower altitudes.
  • Roast Levels: Light, Medium, Dark – impacts flavor and brewing.

3. Coffee Equipment Essentials

Understand how espresso machines work, and know the importance of a quality grinder.

  • Espresso Machine: Boiler, group head, portafilter
  • Grinder: Burr (flat or conical)
  • Tamper, Knock box, Scales

4. Espresso Preparation (SCA Standard)

  • Ratio: 1:2 (e.g., 18g in, 36g out)
  • Time: 25–30 seconds
  • Water Temp: 90.5°C – 96°C
  • Pressure: 9 bars

Always distribute coffee evenly in the basket, tamp firmly and level, and clean group heads regularly.

5. Milk Steaming & Texturing

  • Use cold milk and a clean pitcher
  • Stretch (introduce air) until 37°C
  • Steam to 60–65°C
  • Spin to mix and polish the milk texture

Use steamed milk for lattes, cappuccinos, and flat whites. Proper milk texturing is key to good latte art.

6. Cleaning & Maintenance

Daily cleaning of the grinder, espresso machine, portafilters, steam wand, and surfaces is crucial.

  • Backflush machine daily with water and detergent
  • Clean steam wand after every use
  • Wipe grinders, clean hoppers weekly

7. Basic Sensory Skills

Learn to identify coffee's key sensory attributes:

  • Aroma: Fruity, nutty, floral
  • Body: Light, medium, heavy
  • Acidity: Brightness or liveliness (not sourness)
  • Balance: Harmony between flavor, body, acidity

8. Customer Service & Workflow

A good barista must communicate clearly, be friendly, and understand order flow and time management. Clean work = happy customers + better drinks!
